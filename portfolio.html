2nd Truth Zine

Publication · November 2019

This booklet documents the story and code of an assembly raytracer, arguably the simplest and purest digital method of projecting 3D information into 2D.

The base design was inspired by old computer punchcards, and the yellow pop-ups from tabloid and computer ads— which representic the organic limitations of human vision as opposed to the the pure mathematical nature of the tracer.

Throughout the zine the ads are “infecting” the raytracer with eye abberations and chemicals (like cataracts and rhodopsin), slowing it from finding the “truth” referenced in the title.

On the last page, the raytracer cannot continue ‘getting closer to the truth’ and crashes into the viewer (like a computer program would).

ჰელმანი (Hellman)

Publication · November 2019

Custom Georgian clothing graphic requested and gifted to English music producer Adam Freeland after his biking trip in Mestia, Georgia.

It represents the term “hellman,” meaning someone who is foolhardy, sturdy, and courageous— and also the Grimm Brother’s fairy tale “Briar Rose.” The term refers to the princess in the tale

The design was linocut and printed on masa paper, originally with inverted colors. The type was specially designed for this graphic, incorporating 1970s Georgian hand-painted signage with English blackletter.

The sweatshirt mockup was photographed and made by me.

DIVE Spiked Seltzer

Poster Series · November 2019

DIVE is a fictional brand based on Tycho’s album of the same name. DIVE Spiked Seltzer’s visual motif is made of cascading sets of curved forms, emphasizing inward descent.

The brand offers drinks in 3 flavors, Salt Cherry, Limonchello, and Vanilla. Each can’s design is also randomized with a different “dive” configuration.

Do You Believe in Marlboro?

Publication · November 2019

This poster is about my upbringing with Orthodox Christianity in Georgia. Many people I grew up with were only passively religious, and wore labels like “Christian” and “Masculine” to gain respect from their peers. Often I was brought into social situations where I’d have to prove my “brotherhood” to others, but it felt more like fitting into a prepackaged character.

By dressing up the stereotypical Marlboro cover in Orthodox frames, mixed with 70s–80s Georgian gun/shell packaging I hope to bring these two pressures into one image.

Electro Threads Storefront Redesign

Poster Series · November 2019

Fully modular Shopify theme for mobile and desktop.

Adding things to the homepage with no designer oversight was a must, which necessitated the headers to be pure CSS and look good no matter what image was used. Tools were given within Shopify’s editor to simplify the layout process for every part of the website.

A branding guide was also created for Electro Threads to unify the brand of the company across several designers, outsourced and in-house.
